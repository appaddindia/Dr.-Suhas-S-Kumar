Common Questions and Answers about Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery is a minimally invasive surgical technique where operations are performed through small incisions using a camera and specialized instruments.

Benefits include smaller incisions, less pain, reduced blood loss, shorter hospital stays, quicker recovery, and less scarring.

Common procedures include gallbladder removal, appendectomy, hernia repair, bariatric surgery, hysterectomy, and colorectal surgery.

The duration depends on the type of surgery, but it generally ranges from 30 minutes to a few hours.
